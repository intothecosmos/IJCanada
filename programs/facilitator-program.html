Who Is This For?

The Facilitator Program is designed for people who have established their own personal practice and are ready to support others. You might be drawn to this program if:

  • You've completed at least one of our flagship programs and experienced profound transformation
  • You're called to serve others through facilitating transformation
  • You want to deepen your own practice while developing practical skills
  • You're considering launching a new career in personal development or coaching
  • You're already a coach, therapist, or helper seeking to expand your toolkit
  • You have a strong spiritual or philosophical commitment to service and growth
  • You want to become part of our community of facilitators and builders

Minimum requirement: 1+ year of personal inner work or completion of the Inner Journey Intensive. Serious commitment and readiness to invest in your own development.

What Is the Facilitator Program?

Our Philosophy

We believe that the best facilitators are those who have done their own deep inner work. You can't hold space for transformation if you haven't been through it yourself. Therefore, this program develops the whole person—your personal mastery, your psychological awareness, your spiritual depth, and your practical facilitation skills.

What You'll Develop

Personal Mastery

Continue your own inner work. Identify and transform your edge. Develop the emotional, psychological, and spiritual maturity to hold complex group dynamics with grace.

Facilitation Skills

Learn practical techniques for holding space, managing group dynamics, handling conflict, and creating psychological safety. Theory and applied practice.

Program Knowledge

Deep mastery of our specific programs. Learn the philosophy, content, and delivery methods for the Intensive, 5 Elements, Family Constellation, and other offerings.

Ethical Practice

Develop an ethical foundation for facilitation. Understand boundaries, consent, power dynamics, and responsibility. Build practices that serve the highest good.

Program Structure

The Facilitator Program unfolds over approximately 12–18 months in three integrated phases.

Phase 1: Foundation & Awareness

Duration: 3–4 months | Format: Monthly in-person intensives + ongoing practice

Establish your foundation. Deepen your personal practice. Learn the philosophy and principles underlying all our work. Assess your own edges and growth areas. Develop basic facilitation awareness.

Phase 2: Skill Development & Apprenticeship

Duration: 5–6 months | Format: Bi-weekly meetings + apprenticeship (shadowing facilitators)

Develop practical skills through role-plays, case studies, and real group work. Apprentice with experienced facilitators. Learn how to handle specific situations. Build your confidence and competence. Begin co-facilitating under supervision.

Phase 3: Integration & Graduation

Duration: 4–8 months | Format: Monthly circles + capstone project + independent facilitation

Integrate your learning. Facilitate your first programs independently. Participate in peer consultation groups. Complete a capstone project that demonstrates your mastery. Graduate as a certified facilitator within the Inner Journey Canada community.

Prerequisites & Admission

Minimum Requirements

  • Completion of at least one Inner Journey program (Intensive, 5 Elements, or Family Constellation)
  • OR 1+ year of consistent personal inner work (therapy, meditation, coaching, etc.)
  • Demonstrated emotional maturity and willingness to work on yourself
  • Commitment to attend all required sessions (attendance is crucial)

The Application Process

We select participants through a simple application and interview process. This isn't about excluding people—it's about ensuring the right fit for everyone. We're looking for people who are serious, committed, and genuinely called to this work.

Application includes: A brief written statement about why you want to become a facilitator, what you hope to offer, and any relevant experience. A 30-minute conversation with our director to discuss your readiness and answer questions.

Investment & Time Commitment

This is a significant commitment of time, energy, and resources. Budget approximately 10–15 hours per month during Phase 1, increasing to 15–20 hours during Phase 2 (including apprenticeship), and 10–15 hours during Phase 3. The program runs over 12–18 months.
